From the Army to NASA

After highschool I spent several years in the Army, including service in the US, Germany, and one combat deployment to Iraq. When I left the Army I entered the culinary profession and quickly moved from "dish dog" to Executive Chef. After several years in the culinary field, and becoming a father, I decided to leave the culinary industry and get into CNC machining. I eagerly learned the craft and began to hone my skills as a machinist.

(manual machining at ASUs maching shop during my internship with NASA)

I worked in aerospace manufacturing as a CNC machinist for three years. During this time I quickly learned the intricacies of programming, workholding, tooling, metallurgy, and other skills associated with CNC machining. I then decided to take my career to the next level by persuing an engineering degree.

(sucessfully validated our remote sensing instrument at NASAs Mars Space Flight Facility)

Since I started my engineering degree at Northwestern Michigan College I have excelled academically. I have since been selected for and completed several competetive undergraduate research and design programs with NASA, and was awarded a highly coveted internship with NASA in the summer of 2019. I am currently an engineering student at NMC and continue to work with NASA in a variety of functions, while preparing for a professional career with them.
