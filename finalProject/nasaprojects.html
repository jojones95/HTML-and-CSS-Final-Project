NASA L'SPACE Academy: Mars Lander Design
August 2018 - May 2019

This was my first project with NASA: The Lucy Student Pipeline Accelerator and Compentency Enabler (L'SPACE) Academy. During this Mission Concept Academy, I worked on an engineering team designing, building, and testing a new kind of landing mechanism for small, Martian landers.


NASA Internship: Mars Space Flight Facility
June 2019 - August 2019

During this internship at Arizona State University's School of Earth and Space Exploration, we worked with a team of NASA engineers and scientists as our mentors. We developed remote sensing instruments for asteroid exploration and also performed geological research at the Grand Canyon, Meteor Crater, and Sunset Volcanic Crater.


Ambassador: NASA Lucy Mission to the Trojan Asteriods
July 2019 - Current

As a Lucy Mission Ambassador I work along with scientists from Southwest Research Institute, and NASA's Marshall Space Flight Center to engage the public with lectures and on social media to disseminate information about this mission to Jupiter's Trojan Asteroids.


NASA L'SPACE Academy: NPWEE
August 2019 - Novebmer 2019

The NASA Proposal Writing and Evaluation Experience is the L'SPACE Academy's newest course. During this semester we learned from NASA scientists and engineers how to write effective and professional technical program proposals, and competed for $10,000 in funding from NASA's Marshall Space Flight Center to actually develop our proposed technology.


NASA Community College Aerospace Scholars
October 2019 - Current

The NCAS program puts teams of community college students together to compete in various scientific and technical tasks related to mission design. We spent time learning about NASAs past and future missions, researched one of four different topics and produced an extensive research paper, and are preparing for a robotics competition at a to be determined NASA facility this summer.
